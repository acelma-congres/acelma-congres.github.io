The committee

The conference is organized by a dedicated team of female students from the École de technologie supérieure (ÉTS) from different backgrounds, united by a common vision: to pave the way for engineering that is more inclusive, equitable, and representative of society.

Presidency

Sarah Thammavongsa

Sarah is a construction engineering student at ÉTS. She is president of the organizing committee for the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. After participating in last year's conference organized by Génie au Féminin, she wanted to continue this wonderful initiative. Since arriving at ÉTS, she has been actively involved in the cause of women in engineering, particularly with Les Ingénieuses. She believes it is important to set an example for future female engineers and remind them that anything is possible, regardless of their background.

VP Events

Yasmina Kawsar

Yasmina is a student in operations and logistics engineering at ÉTS. She is the vice president of events for the organizing committee of the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. She got involved in this project because she deeply believes in the importance of creating a space where women in engineering can come together, inspire each other, and support one another. Convinced of the power of networking and sharing experiences, she hopes that this conference will provide a memorable opportunity for participants, like herself, to recognize each other, encourage each other, and look forward to their future in engineering with ambition.

VP Logistics

Raimoemoea Boyer

Raimoemoea is a software engineering student at ÉTS. She is the vice president of logistics for the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. By getting involved in this event, she hopes to actively contribute to promoting the role of women in engineering, a field that is still heavily influenced by men.

VP Partnership

Aya Rehimi

Aya is a student in operations and logistics engineering at ÉTS. She is the vice president of partnerships for the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. She wanted to get involved in order to strengthen ties between the engineering field and its partners. She chose this role to contribute to the creation of an event that brings people together, inspires, and advances the role of women in engineering.

VP Finance

Lilian Elrachdi

Lilian is a student in operations and logistics engineering at ÉTS. She serves as vice president of finance on the organizing committee for the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. She wants to help create an event that lives up to the inspiration these women represent. For Lilian, this project is a wonderful opportunity to highlight the richness of women's careers in engineering, to create a supportive and inspiring space, and to contribute to a community where every voice counts. Through her role, Lilian hopes to encourage other women to believe in their place in this field.

VP Marketing

Emilina Maggiore

Emilina, a civil engineering student at ÉTS, is vice-president of communications for the organizing committee of the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. She chose to get involved in order to actively contribute to women's engagement in the field of engineering and encourage their presence in an environment where they are still too often underrepresented. For her, every initiative that highlights the careers of women in engineering is a way to pave the way for future generations.

VP Visual

Yasmine Beddouch

After completing a University Pathway in Technology (CUT), Yasmine is now studying software engineering at ÉTS and serves as visual vice president for the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. Very involved in the ÉTS community, she is an ambassador, assistant for feminist affairs for the AÉÉTS, and a member of numerous student clubs. She wanted to get involved in order to highlight the contribution of women in engineering and inspire other women.

Co VP Visual

Annelise Celma

Annelise is a software engineering student. She serves as co-vice president of visuals on the organizing committee. She chose this role because she understands the importance of visual identity in conveying a strong message and standing out in an environment that is still considered very male-dominated. She hopes that this initiative will shine a spotlight on women in engineering, encouraging them to believe in themselves and dare to take risks.

VP IT

Selma Boulkamh

Selma is pursuing a master's degree in software engineering at ÉTS and serves as vice president of IT on the organizing committee for the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. Selma chose to get involved in the conference because, for her, it is much more than just an event. It is a human and collective adventure, where every interaction and every idea shared has the power to create unique connections. It is an opportunity to transform dreams and ambitions into a vibrant story that can touch hearts, inspire, and bring people together.

VP Writing

Alicia Guerfi

Alicia, a student in operations and logistics engineering at ÉTS, is the vice president of writing for the organizing committee of the 2026 Women in Engineering Conference. She wanted to get involved in the conference to bring the stories of women in engineering to life, because she believes that a well-told story can inspire others to get started. Behind every female engineer is a journey worth sharing !
